LOCAL DELICACIES

Treats that will lighten up your day

CALAMAY (SANKAKA)

Calamay, also known as sankaka, is a sticky sweet delicacy that is popular in many regions of the Philippines, including Polangui, Albay. It is made of coconut milk, brown sugar, and ground glutinous rice. The mixture is cooked until it thickens and becomes sticky, then molded into small bite-sized pieces. Calamay is often served as a dessert or snack and is a favorite among locals and tourists alike. m

IBOS

Ibos refers to a type of rice cake in the Philippines, particularly in Bicolano cuisine, which is known for its unique use of coconut and rice. Suman sa Ibos is a Filipino snack made with glutinous rice soaked with lye water overnight. This is exactly the same way as making suman-sa-lihiya apart from how they are wrapped up. Suman-sa-Lihiya is wrapped in banana leaves while the latter is wrapped with coconut leaves or buri/palm tree leaves. The best when paired with hot brewed coffee or “Kapeng Barako”.

BALINSUSO

Balinsuso is a traditional Filipino delicacy, specifically a rice cake from the Philippines. The term "balinsuso" literally translates to "sucking the breast" in English, and it is called so because of its breast-like appearance. It's often served during special occasions and festivals. Balinsuso is made from glutinous rice flour, sugar, and coconut milk, which are mixed to form a dough. The dough is then shaped into small mounds resembling breasts and steamed until cooked.

BINUTONG

Binutong is made up of glutinous rice with coconut cream that is wrapped in banana leaves. This is often served during breakfast or merienda, and is best paired with hot chocolate or coffee. The term itself came from the root word, "butok" which means to "tie the knot" and that is why the banana leaves were wrapped and tied it for the flavor of the binutong to give off a savory and wonderful flavor so that when it was eaten, you can feel the binutong melts in your mouth and at the same time, the chewy goodness of it is also there.

SINAPOT

Sinapot is a local term for maruya. These are sliced bananas dipped in batter, deep fried and dredged in sugar. All are popular street food and is best eaten during merienda.

BINIRIBID

Biniribid is a popular Filipino delicacy made from glutinous rice flour, sugar, and coconut milk. It is twisted into a shape similar to a rope, deep-fried until golden brown, and often coated in sugar for added sweetness. Biniribid is a delightful and chewy snack enjoyed.
