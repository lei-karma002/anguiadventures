POLANGUI

The history of POLANGUI

Polangui is a first class municipality in the province of Albay, Philippines. It is now a thriving, progressive first-class growth center, considered as the most promising and potential economic destination of the flourishing trade and commercial activities of the Third District of Albay.

The origin of the name Polangui has many versions. The foremost and most accepted version is “that of a giant, robust tree that existed majestically in the early municipal settlement”. The natives of the settlement called it “Oyangue”, which also served as the early landmark of the area where new settlers looked upon it in their wandering. Similarly, the early Spanish frontier settlers found this tree, and it became their famous settlement landmark. The settlement was then called “Binanuaan” but was often referred to as “Oyangue” by nearby settlers. As more settlers came to dwell and engage in various trade endeavors with the indigenous dwellers, the more the settlement was known in distant areas by the name “Oyangue” referring to the tree landmark that was more widely accepted than “Binanuaan”. The passing of generations corrupted the word “Oyangue” into several acronyms. Most acceptable and widely used before were “Polangue” and later "Polangui,” which have remained today.

Another legend is about the story of a maiden named "Pulang Angui," which means "Red Maria" (Angui is the nickname for Maria), who loved red colors for dress and whose beautiful body, face, and red lips became the object of affection by the males to the point of adoration. She was modest in her ways, talented for possessing various skills, and had a happy disposition, showing a love of the arts and religion. She would lead the tribe in the festivities. When the Spaniards came, the soldiers who first set foot in Polangui asked for the name of the place. The native thought the foreigners were asking for the name "Pulang Angui" and said so. The Spaniards recorded the name of the place as Pulangui, a concoction of the name that was later, as the years went by, converted to Polangui. Geography.

Festival

Pulang-Angui Festival

The Polangui Town Fiesta, which started as "Oyangui Festival" in 2003 and later became "Pulang Angui Festival", is an annual celebration of Polangueños which tells the story of a maiden who loves everything in red. “Red Maria” (Angui is the nickname for Maria) who loved red colors for dress and whose beautiful body, face and red lips became the object of affection by the males to the point of adoration.

During the whole month of June. a flood of tourists visit the town as Polangueños optimistically await and generously prepare for the month-long celebration as the festivities feature various religious, cultural, social, and sports activities that capture the delight, excitement, and imagination of the people and visitors during the occasion.

But as the dates of June 28 and 29 come, Polangueños temporarily change the happy to a more serious mood to commemorate the solemnity of the parish patrons, Sts. Peter and Paul through a concelebrated mass. Some elders often prefer to gather at the chapels to lead Novena Masses.

Pageant

Pulang-Angui Pageant

Pageantry plays an important role in the lives of all Polangueños, the festival's centerpiece event, which displays a vibrant public procession, show, and ceremony in terms of beauty, talents, and intelligence. The thrill and excitement of knowing who is going to reign as the “Mutya ng Pulang- Angui,” a queen with modest in her ways, talented for possessing various skills, with a happy disposition, showing love of arts and religion.

Cultural Presentation

Pulang-Angui Culture

They displayed and held a street dance competition in which the dance told the story of a warrior who fell in love with a woman in red (the Red Lady). But there was an evil witch who also loved the warrior, and she cursed the Red Lady (or killed her?) and bewitched the warrior. Then, a good fairy came and released the Red Lady from her curse and drove away the witch, and the Red Lady and the warrior lived happily ever after.

Trade Fair

Pulang-Angui Trade Fair

Farmers Trade fairs are important events for businesses in Polangueños to showcase their products and services, and to connect with potential customers and partners. Polangui produce a variety of products, including sugarcane products, rice, corn, cacao, pili, root crops, vegetables, coconuts, abacá, and Pandan-based products.

They are also known for its sugarcane industry, and one of the most popular products is muscovado sugar. Panutsa, a solid, brown-colored unrefined cane sugar, is one of the major agricultural products of Polangui. Balangibang Palayamanan Farmers Inc. is a company in Polangui that produces fruit and tree nut farming products.

Sports Fest

Pulang-Angui Sports Fest

The festival features various sports activities that capture the delight, excitement, and imagination of the people and visitors during the occasion. The most popular are basket balls, mobile games, motocross, and other games that most of the people in the municipality of Polangui join and enjoy.

Dance workshop/ Talent Show

Pulang-Angui Talent

The LGU conducting talent shows can be seen as a way to help boost the self-esteem, confidence, and assurance of youth. Some communities and companies see talent shows as a way to help prevent juvenile delinquency among children, teens, and young adults. A good example is the best zumba dancer competition in the municipality.
