POLANGUI

The history of POLANGUI

Polangui is a first class municipality in the province of Albay, Philippines. It is now a thriving, progressive first-class growth center, considered as the most promising and potential economic destination of the flourishing trade and commercial activities of the Third District of Albay.

The origin of the name Polangui has many versions. The foremost and most accepted version is “that of a giant, robust tree that existed majestically in the early municipal settlement”. The natives of the settlement called it “Oyangue”, which also served as the early landmark of the area where new settlers looked upon it in their wandering. Similarly, the early Spanish frontier settlers found this tree, and it became their famous settlement landmark. The settlement was then called “Binanuaan” but was often referred to as “Oyangue” by nearby settlers. As more settlers came to dwell and engage in various trade endeavors with the indigenous dwellers, the more the settlement was known in distant areas by the name “Oyangue” referring to the tree landmark that was more widely accepted than “Binanuaan”. The passing of generations corrupted the word “Oyangue” into several acronyms. Most acceptable and widely used before were “Polangue” and later "Polangui,” which have remained today.

Another legend is about the story of a maiden named "Pulang Angui," which means "Red Maria" (Angui is the nickname for Maria), who loved red colors for dress and whose beautiful body, face, and red lips became the object of affection by the males to the point of adoration. She was modest in her ways, talented for possessing various skills, and had a happy disposition, showing a love of the arts and religion. She would lead the tribe in the festivities. When the Spaniards came, the soldiers who first set foot in Polangui asked for the name of the place. The native thought the foreigners were asking for the name "Pulang Angui" and said so. The Spaniards recorded the name of the place as Pulangui, a concoction of the name that was later, as the years went by, converted to Polangui. Geography.

Tourist Destinations

Natural Park

Lake Danao Natural Park in Barangay Danao is a small, scenic crater lake, located 15 kilometers from the town proper. It is nestled between Mt. Malinao and Mt. Masaraga. The rare tabios or sinarapan (Mystichtys luzonensis), the world’s smallest fish, is also cultured here. The lake is a 30-minute hike through carabao trails and abaca plantations. Better bring a guide.

Danao Lake offers an exhilirating environment, it is surrounded by lush and verdant vegetation and is haven for anlers asbig eels and other fresh water fish abound. Located at barangay Danao, Polangui, Albay with a land elevation of about 500 meters above sea level and with a distance of about 20 km. from the town.

Transportation

Legazpi City Legazpi Airport

The nearest airport is in Legazpi City Legazpi Airport – about 39.1 kilometres (24.3 mi) from Polangui. Philippine Airlines and Cebu Pacific Air operate in the airport, with daily flights to and from Manila and Cebu. The construction of the soon Polangui Domestic Airport at Barangay Balangibang is still under negotiation.

LOCAL DELICACIES

Treats that will lighten up your day

CALAMAY (SANKAKA)

Calamay, also known as sankaka, is a sticky sweet delicacy that is popular in many regions of the Philippines, including Polangui, Albay. It is made of coconut milk, brown sugar, and ground glutinous rice. The mixture is cooked until it thickens and becomes sticky, then molded into small bite-sized pieces. Calamay is often served as a dessert or snack and is a favorite among locals and tourists alike. m

IBOS

Ibos refers to a type of rice cake in the Philippines, particularly in Bicolano cuisine, which is known for its unique use of coconut and rice. Suman sa Ibos is a Filipino snack made with glutinous rice soaked with lye water overnight. This is exactly the same way as making suman-sa-lihiya apart from how they are wrapped up. Suman-sa-Lihiya is wrapped in banana leaves while the latter is wrapped with coconut leaves or buri/palm tree leaves. The best when paired with hot brewed coffee or “Kapeng Barako”.

BALINSUSO

Balinsuso is a traditional Filipino delicacy, specifically a rice cake from the Philippines. The term "balinsuso" literally translates to "sucking the breast" in English, and it is called so because of its breast-like appearance. It's often served during special occasions and festivals. Balinsuso is made from glutinous rice flour, sugar, and coconut milk, which are mixed to form a dough. The dough is then shaped into small mounds resembling breasts and steamed until cooked.

BINUTONG

Binutong is made up of glutinous rice with coconut cream that is wrapped in banana leaves. This is often served during breakfast or merienda, and is best paired with hot chocolate or coffee. The term itself came from the root word, "butok" which means to "tie the knot" and that is why the banana leaves were wrapped and tied it for the flavor of the binutong to give off a savory and wonderful flavor so that when it was eaten, you can feel the binutong melts in your mouth and at the same time, the chewy goodness of it is also there.

SINAPOT

Sinapot is a local term for maruya. These are sliced bananas dipped in batter, deep fried and dredged in sugar. All are popular street food and is best eaten during merienda.

BINIRIBID

Biniribid is a popular Filipino delicacy made from glutinous rice flour, sugar, and coconut milk. It is twisted into a shape similar to a rope, deep-fried until golden brown, and often coated in sugar for added sweetness. Biniribid is a delightful and chewy snack enjoyed.
